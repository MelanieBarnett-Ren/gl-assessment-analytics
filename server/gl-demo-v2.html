  • This compares unfavourably against 23% of students for whom maths attainment is above average or very high.

  • Students are performing 4 SAS points below their CAT4 potential, indicating negative value added.

  • Focus on Number - fractions strand: Review the strand analysis showing Y5 National Curriculum objectives. Target Focus Skills 524, 526 & 531 (add/subtract fractions with same denominator; write decimals as fractions) - these are among the essential 23% of skills that underpin future learning.
  • Analyse class-level variation: Use the cross-class heatmap to identify which classes (5A, 5B, 5C) need targeted support on specific strands.
  • Review students not making expected progress: Identify the 12 students in "Foundation" focus group who need intensive intervention. View Class 5A students ›
  • Investigate value-added gap: Use CAT4 vs NGMT analysis to identify students underperforming relative to potential. View scatter plot ›
  • Support EAL and SEND learners: Look into the scores for students with EAL and SEND – to identify specific curriculum barriers and adapt teaching strategies.
  • Address declining trend: Review 4-term trend data to identify which domains are declining fastest (Number -8 SAS, Algebra -5 SAS).
  • Learn from School C: School C has similar demographics but scores 17 SAS points higher. Request collaborative visits to observe their fractions teaching strategies ›
